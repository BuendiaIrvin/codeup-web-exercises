Ten National Park Facts

Yellowstone National Park
The oldest U.S. national park, founded in 1872.
Ribbon Falls in Yosemite
Is nine times taller than Niagara Falls.
Death Valley National Park
One of the hottest temperatures on earth was recorded in 1913, registering 134 degrees.
The Grand Canyon
Known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.
California's Sequoia National Park
home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world, the wonderfully named General Sherman. The tree is approximately 275 feet tall and weighs approximately 1,900 metric tons.
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park
is the largest national park, covering 13,000 square miles or 13.2 million acres.
Hot Springs National Park in Arkansas
coming in at about 5,500 acres, it is the smallest national park. This was actually the first federally protected piece of land in 1832. It was not named a national park until 1921.
Great Smoky Mountains National Park
is the most visited national park with more than 9 million guests per year, followed by Grand Canyon National Park, which gets more than 4 million visits per year.
Denali National Park
Mount McKinley in the Alaska Range, is the highest point in the national parks at 20,302 feet.
Arches National Park
is famous for having the largest concentration of stone arches in the world.
